Varanasi is a city in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh dating to the 11th century B.C. Regarded as the spiritual capital of India, the city draws Hindu pilgrims who bathe in the Ganges River’s sacred waters and perform funeral rites.

Ghats

Ghats in Varanasi are riverfront steps leading to the banks of the River Ganges. The city has 88 ghats. Most of the ghats are bathing and puja ceremony ghats, while two ghats are used exclusively as cremation sites.

Universities

Indian Institute of Technology, Varanasi. Institute of Medical Sciences, Banaras Hindu University (IMS BHU) School of Management Sciences, Varanasi. Kashi Institute of Technology (KIT), Varanasi. SAMS Institute of Hotel Management, Varanasi. Rajarshi School of Management & Technology (RSM), Varanasi.

Food Places

Varanasi is famous for its kachoris, chaat, lassi and, of course, paan. Varanasi is famous for its own style of chaat: dahi bhalla, tamatar ki chaat and palak chaat. In the lane just beyond the famous Kashi Vishwanath Temple is Kachori Gali.
